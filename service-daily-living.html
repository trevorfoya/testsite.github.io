Daily Living Skills

Building the structure, routine and practical skills needed for a fulfilling, independent life.

Building skills for everyday life

Our daily living skills programme helps service users develop the structure and routines that underpin wellbeing and independence. We focus on comfort, security and the habits that foster confidence.

What we cover

  • Getting up on time and making their bed
  • Personal hygiene and self-care routines
  • Getting dressed and presenting themselves appropriately
  • Preparing meals and maintaining a balanced diet
  • Support with benefits and financial entitlements
  • Handling cash and basic banking
  • Budgeting and managing household finances
  • Shopping and planning ahead
  • Cooking and washing up
  • Laundry, ironing and storing clothes
  • Verbal and written communication skills

Our approach

Staff work alongside service users patiently and positively, recognising that these skills can take time to develop — especially for people who have experienced significant disruption in their lives. Progress is celebrated, however small.
