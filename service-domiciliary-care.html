Domiciliary Care (Home-to-Home)

Our new home-to-home care service supports people to live safely and independently in their own homes, with flexible support tailored to individual needs.

New service — CQC Regulated

What is domiciliary care?

Domiciliary care — also known as home care — is professional support provided to people in their own homes. Rather than requiring someone to move into a residential setting, our service brings experienced, compassionate support workers directly to the service user.

Whether it is for a few hours or round the clock, our care and support services are flexible, friendly, professional and high quality — always. We can be relied on to be on time, even at short notice.

Assessments are free, with no hidden costs. Simply talk to us and we can put together a plan that suits you.

Who is this service for?

  • Adults with mental health difficulties living independently
  • Individuals transitioning from supported living to their own home
  • People recovering from substance misuse needing ongoing low-level support
  • Adults with complex needs who prefer to remain in their community
  • Individuals with a personal budget who want to direct their own care

Our specialisms

  • Dementia
  • Mental health conditions
  • Physical disabilities
  • Learning disabilities
  • Sensory impairments

Support services we offer

  • Personal care
  • Companionship
  • Assistance with medication
  • Assistance with shopping
  • Meal preparation
  • Light housekeeping
  • Community participation

If your area is not listed, please contact us — we may still be able to help or signpost you appropriately.

How it works

Our care packages will be based on your healthcare needs. We can assist you as often as you would like — for short periods, around the clock, or live-in. Our packages are fully flexible and can range from a few hours per week to several visits per day, depending on assessed needs.

We pride ourselves on our ability to listen to our service users and being responsive to their challenges and changes in their needs. We respect individual choice and decision-making and foster a culture of tolerance and inclusion in everything we do.

Why choose IHD Care for domiciliary care?

  • CQC regulated — inspected and assessed against strict standards
  • Experienced team with specialist mental health knowledge
  • Fully flexible packages — from 1 hour per week upwards, including live-in care
  • Free assessments with no hidden costs
  • Seamless transition support from our supported living services
  • Close working relationships with CMHT, GPs and Social Services
  • Personal budget support available
  • Support for people from diverse backgrounds with a wide range of needs
