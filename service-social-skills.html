Social Skills Development

Helping service users build the confidence and social skills they need to thrive in the community.

Developing social confidence

Our social skills programme works with service users to develop the interpersonal abilities and community confidence that are essential for independent living. Staff work closely with each individual to understand their starting point and tailor support accordingly.

Areas we work on

  • Assertiveness — protecting against vulnerability and abuse
  • Confidence building in everyday situations
  • Going out and about in the community
  • Dressing appropriately for different occasions
  • Communication skills — both verbal and non-verbal
  • Appropriate behaviour in different settings
  • Managing alcohol and drug use (safe limits and recovery programmes)
  • Health and wellbeing awareness
  • Managing relationships with family and professionals

How this helps

Building social skills is foundational to every other aspect of independent living. When service users have the confidence and ability to engage with their community, manage relationships and advocate for themselves, outcomes across all areas of life improve significantly.
